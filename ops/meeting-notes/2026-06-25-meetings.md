# Meetings — 2026-06-25

## Summary
The meeting finalized UI designs for AI-powered meeting features, covering meeting state tags, recap content structure, and prep brief output. Key decisions were made on tag states for upcoming and past meetings, content sections for recap vs. prep brief, and the design workflow split between Claude (prototyping) and Figma (source of truth for developers). Designs were shown to Doug in a monthly meeting; he liked the feature with little feedback.

## Key decisions
- Meeting state tags for upcoming meetings: AI Prep Ready, Upload Transcript to Update AI Insights (non-Zoom only), Processing, AI Updates Available – Reload, and AI Updated — five states total.
- "Upload Transcript" tag will include a small link that scrolls to and opens the AI Transcripts section automatically.
- Past meetings also show Upload Transcript tag where applicable; AI Prep Ready is not applicable for past meetings.
- "Completed" tag and "Meeting Recap [date]" label to be removed from past meetings view.
- Recap content structure: Summary (few lines), Blockers (max 3), What Happened (sub-items: decided, agreed, open risks), Follow-ups (max 5); outcome cap scales by duration (<=30 min = 3 points, 30-60 min = 5 points).
- Prep brief content: ARC Synthesis (1-2 sentences of text), Attention Flags (bullet items), Goal Signals — displayed without explicit section title labels; just bullet points.
- "AI Suggested" header block to be removed; AI logo retained on individual line items only.
- Design workflow: Claude for flows/prototypes; Figma as source of truth for styling/spacing/developer handoff — to prevent mismatch and double rework.
- Uploaded transcripts displayed read-only (format unknown for manual uploads); Zoom transcripts follow timestamp/name/note format.
- Goals form will load inline across the platform (not restricted to meetings view); redirect is a stopgap.
